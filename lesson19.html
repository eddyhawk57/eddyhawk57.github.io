Lesson 19: Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)

What are RNNs?

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) are deep learning models designed to work with sequential data â€” data where order matters. They have a form of memory, making them suitable for tasks like time-series forecasting, speech recognition, and language modeling.

Why Use RNNs?

  • They can retain information from previous inputs to influence current output.
  • Ideal for problems involving sequences (text, audio, stock prices).

Challenges with RNNs

  • They can suffer from vanishing/exploding gradients.
  • Training is slower due to sequential nature.
  • Long-term dependencies are hard to capture without enhancements like LSTM or GRU.

Basic RNN with Keras


from keras.models import Sequential
from keras.layers import SimpleRNN, Dense

model = Sequential()
model.add(SimpleRNN(50, activation='relu', input_shape=(timesteps, features)))
model.add(Dense(1))

model.compile(optimizer='adam', loss='mse')
model.fit(X_train, y_train, epochs=10, batch_size=32)
